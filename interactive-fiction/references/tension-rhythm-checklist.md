# Tension Rhythm Checklist

Use this checklist when a thriller route starts feeling flat, breathless, or mechanically loud.

## Pressure Wave

- A touched route shows a readable rise-fall pattern instead of stacking the same pressure value repeatedly.
- Investigation beats can serve as oxygen if they sharpen method instead of repeating mood.
- A spike beat changes the social or logical state of the room, not just the tone.

## Choice Rhythm

- The route gives the player at least one lower-pressure beat to understand what the previous spike changed.
- Immediate consequence scenes do not all peak at `high` or `spike`.
- A quieter beat still advances suspicion, clue logic, or leverage.

## Doctor Expectations

- No reachable route should stack three `high` or `spike` beats in a row.
- Routes that spike should show at least one later `low` or `medium` beat before they end.
- Pressure stats should make it easy to spot the worst high-run across the current graph.

## Red-Team Layer

- A hostile reading can name where the route feels exhausting instead of suspenseful.
- The revision can point to one concrete breather beat and one concrete spike beat.
- The next band can name which pressure pattern still needs work.
