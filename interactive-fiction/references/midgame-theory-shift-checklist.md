# Midgame Theory Shift Checklist

Use this checklist when the thriller's middle game needs to do more than extend investigation.

## Theory Revision Contract

- At least one reachable midgame node declares `thriller.theoryRevisions`.
- A theory revision names the suspect the player is moving away from and the suspect now entering play.
- The revision is caused by a visible clue, document, contradiction, or social break, not by summary alone.

## Player Legibility

- The player can tell what working theory just became unstable.
- The route does not hide the new suspect logic behind vague atmosphere.
- The revised scene preserves suspense while making the changed theory more actionable.

## Structural Discipline

- Do not jump directly from clue reveal to ending if the theory needs one more proving beat.
- Add a compact reversal node when a branch currently changes theory too quickly.
- Preserve route memory when a theory-shift branch merges back into a shared scene.

## Doctor Expectations

- The doctor can count reachable theory revision nodes.
- The doctor can name how many unique suspects are targeted by reachable theory revisions.
- Broken revision annotations fail fast when they reference missing characters or self-to-self shifts.

## Red-Team Layer

- A hostile reading can name where the middle game merely extends suspicion instead of changing it.
- The revision can point to one concrete scene where the player's leading theory now wobbles.
- The next band can name whether the new theory shift still lacks downstream payoff.
