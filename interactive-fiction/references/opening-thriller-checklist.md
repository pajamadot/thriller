# Opening Thriller Checklist

Use this when evolving the first band of an interactive thriller.

The opening is working when:

- the first two player-facing nodes state a real mystery or threat
- the first major choice exposes at least two distinct intentions
- the opening window contains a pressure spike
- at least one early clue is visible before any major accusation
- the first branch creates a visible difference within one to three later nodes
- any merge created by the opening remembers what the player did

## Practical Pattern

For a compact playable thriller opening, the following rhythm is reliable:

1. Opening contract scene
2. Pressure spike investigation scene
3. Route-colored aftermath scene
4. Shared pressure scene or early ending

This keeps the player from reading too long before interaction while avoiding a fake branch that collapses immediately.

## Typical Failure Modes

- both opening choices feel like the same social move
- the player can accuse before earning leverage
- the opening tease has no later payoff
- a merge forgets whether the player complied or defied
- suspense relies on atmosphere alone instead of visible pressure
