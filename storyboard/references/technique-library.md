### A01: Psycho -- Shower Scene

**Film**: Psycho (1960)
**Director**: Alfred Hitchcock | **Year**: 1960 | **Genre**: Horror/Thriller
**Scene**: Marion Crane is attacked in the shower. 45 seconds, 70+ cuts.

#### Shot Breakdown (estimated)

| # | Size | Angle | Movement | Duration | Content |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 2.0s | Marion steps into shower, pulls curtain |
| 2 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 1.5s | Marion under water, relaxed face |
| 3 | CU | LOW | STATIC | 1.2s | Showerhead spraying water (POV) |
| 4 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 1.0s | Shadow appears behind curtain |
| 5 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 0.8s | Marion's face, unaware |
| 6 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 0.5s | Curtain ripped open, silhouette with knife raised |
| 7 | ECU | HIGH | STATIC | 0.3s | Marion's mouth -- scream |
| 8 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 0.4s | Knife blade (never shown penetrating) |
| 9 | MCU | HIGH | STATIC | 0.3s | Marion's torso recoiling |
| 10 | ECU | EYE | STATIC | 0.3s | Hand against tile wall |
| 11 | CU | LOW | STATIC | 0.3s | Knife arcing downward |
| 12 | MCU | DUTCH | STATIC | 0.4s | Marion's face contorted |
| 13 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 0.3s | Knife from different angle |
| 14 | ECU | HIGH | STATIC | 0.3s | Abdomen/hip area |
| 15 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 0.3s | Knife again |
| 16 | MCU | DUTCH | STATIC | 0.4s | Marion grabbing at attacker |
| 17-50 | ECU/CU | VARIOUS | STATIC | 0.2-0.5s | Rapid alternation: knife/body/face/hands/water/tile -- never showing actual penetration |
| 51 | CU | HIGH | STATIC | 0.5s | Marion's hand sliding down tile |
| 52 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 1.0s | Attacker retreats, silhouette exits |
| 53 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 1.5s | Marion slides down, grabs curtain |
| 54 | MCU | HIGH | STATIC | 1.0s | Curtain rings popping off rod one by one |
| 55 | MS | HIGH | STATIC | 1.5s | Marion falls out of tub onto floor |
| 56 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 3.0s | Water running, mixing with blood |
| 57 | ECU | BIRD | STATIC | 2.0s | Drain -- water spiraling with blood |
| 58 | ECU | EYE | SLOW ZOOM OUT | 4.0s | Match dissolve: drain spiral becomes Marion's dead eye |
| 59 | CU | EYE | SLOW PULL | 3.0s | Marion's lifeless eye, camera slowly pulls back to reveal her face on floor |

**Total**: ~70 cuts in ~45 seconds (attack portion: shots 6-55), decelerating to ~3 cuts in final 10 seconds.

#### Logic Analysis

- **Cut reasons**: The attack sequence (shots 6-55) almost entirely abandons INFORMATION and REACTION as cut reasons. Instead, the cuts serve a **fifth purpose not in our framework**: SENSATION -- the cut itself is the content. The rapid fragmentation does not show new information (we never see the knife enter flesh), does not show reactions in the traditional sense. It uses the edit itself as an assault on the viewer's perceptual system.
- **Information flow**: The DAG is intentionally broken. REQUIRES chains are violated -- shots reference nothing from previous shots. PROVIDES are redundant (knife, body, knife, body). This is the point: the DAG breakdown mirrors the breakdown of the victim's bodily integrity.
- **Idioms used**: None of the standard idioms apply during the attack. The establishing sequence (shots 1-5) uses a modified ESTABLISH idiom. The aftermath (shots 51-59) uses a novel DECELERATION-REVEAL pattern.
- **Post-attack**: Shots 56-59 deploy the only conventional logic: the drain shot PROVIDES the visual metaphor (life draining away), the match dissolve to the eye PROVIDES the confirmation of death. These final shots have the highest INFO_DENSITY of the sequence.

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T01 -- Fragmentation Violence
**Idiom映射**: 此技法提出了新 Idiom (FRAGMENTATION-ASSAULT)，同时反转了 CHASE idiom 的加速逻辑——以碎片化取代速度作为感知武器。见 shot-logic.md。
**Pattern**: ESTABLISH(calm, 2-4 shots, normal duration) -> INTRUSION(1 shot, shock) -> FRAGMENT(N shots, duration < 0.5s, no continuity, never showing the actual violence) -> DECELERATE(3-5 shots, duration increasing) -> SYMBOL(1-2 shots, metaphoric closure)
**Parameters**:
- `N_fragments`: 30-70 (more = more overwhelming)
- `min_fragment_duration`: 0.2-0.5s
- `continuity_preservation`: 0% to 30% (lower = more disorienting)
- `actual_violence_shown`: 0% (key constraint -- the edit implies what is never shown)
- `deceleration_ratio`: final shot duration / average fragment duration (target: 10x-20x)
- `symbolic_closure`: drain/eye/object match cut
**Emotional Effect**: Visceral horror without graphic content. The viewer's brain assembles the violence from fragments, making it more disturbing than explicit depiction.
**When to Use**: Murder scenes, assault scenes, traumatic events where the subjective experience of violation matters more than the literal action.
**When NOT to Use**: Scenes requiring spatial clarity. Scenes where the audience needs to understand who did what to whom (whodunit reveals). Dialogue scenes. Any scene lasting more than 60 seconds at this intensity.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A01**: Our framework has no concept of "cut as content" -- a fifth cut reason where the edit rhythm itself carries meaning independent of what any individual shot contains.
- Current framework: every cut needs INFORMATION/REACTION/EMPHASIS/ORIENTATION
- Psycho shower: cuts serve SENSATION -- the assault on perception IS the narrative
- **Proposed fix**: Add optional fifth cut reason `SENSATION` to shot-logic.md for sequences where the editing pattern itself is the primary information carrier. Constrain: `SENSATION` cuts must occur in bursts (>5 consecutive) and require explicit entry/exit from the SENSATION mode via a standard-logic shot.

### A02: Rear Window -- Opening Pan

**Film**: Rear Window (1954)
**Director**: Alfred Hitchcock | **Year**: 1954 | **Genre**: Thriller/Mystery
**Scene**: Opening shot -- a single continuous camera movement establishes protagonist, setting, backstory, and thematic premise in ~2.5 minutes without dialogue.

#### Shot Breakdown (estimated)

This is a single continuous shot with internal movements. I break it into "sub-shots" defined by the camera's resting positions.

| # | Size | Angle | Movement | Duration | Content |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1a | ELS | EYE | STATIC->PAN R | 8s | Three windows of courtyard apartments, morning activity |
| 1b | MS | EYE | PAN R (continuing) | 5s | Neighbor exercising on balcony, cat on stairs |
| 1c | LS | EYE | PAN R->TILT DOWN | 6s | Street below, milkman, daily routine |
| 1d | LS | EYE | PAN R (continuing) | 5s | More apartments -- couple sleeping on balcony (heat) |
| 1e | MS | EYE | PAN R->STOP | 4s | Thermometer on wall -- 92 degrees (info plant) |
| 1f | MS-CU | EYE | PAN L + TILT DOWN | 5s | Jefferies sleeping, sweating -- face in CU |
| 1g | CU | EYE | PAN DOWN | 3s | His leg -- full cast, from hip to ankle |
| 1h | CU | EYE | PAN R | 3s | Beside the cast: itching stick, table of medications |
| 1i | MCU | EYE | PAN R | 4s | Smashed camera on side table |
| 1j | CU | EYE | PAN R | 3s | Photograph -- dramatic crash at a racetrack (print) |
| 1k | CU | EYE | PAN R | 3s | Another photograph -- explosion, war zone |
| 1l | CU | EYE | PAN R | 3s | Stack of magazine covers -- negative of glamorous woman |
| 1m | MCU | EYE | PAN R->TILT UP | 4s | Positive print of same woman -- reveals it's a magazine cover model |
| 1n | LS | EYE | PAN R to window | 6s | Camera returns to courtyard view -- full circle completed |

**Total**: 1 continuous shot, ~62 seconds (some accounts say up to 2:30 including slight pauses). Zero cuts.

#### Logic Analysis

- **Cut reasons**: There are NO cuts. The camera movement replaces editing entirely. Each pan/tilt to a new subject serves the function of a cut but without the perceptual disruption.
- **Information flow**: Perfectly linear DAG despite zero cuts. Each "sub-shot" PROVIDES information that the next one REQUIRES:
  - 1a-1d: PROVIDES world (courtyard, neighbors, heat) -> REQUIRES context for...
  - 1e: PROVIDES 92 degrees -> REQUIRES the visual of sweating people -> explains why...
  - 1f: PROVIDES sleeping sweating man -> REQUIRES heat context -> sets up why...
  - 1g: PROVIDES broken leg -> REQUIRES his immobility context -> explains why...
  - 1h-1i: PROVIDES medications + smashed camera -> REQUIRES broken leg -> tells us how...
  - 1j-1k: PROVIDES action photos -> REQUIRES smashed camera -> establishes profession...
  - 1l-1m: PROVIDES magazine/model -> REQUIRES photographer profession -> hints at romance subplot...
  - 1n: PROVIDES return to courtyard -> REQUIRES all prior info -> now we understand WHY he watches
- **Idioms used**: This is a single-shot ESTABLISH idiom taken to its logical extreme. Where the standard idiom is ELS->LS->MS (3 shots), Hitchcock achieves ELS->LS->MS->CU->INSERT->CU (whole journey) in one shot.

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T02 -- Environmental Exposition Pan
**Idiom映射**: 此技法提出了新 Idiom (ENVIRONMENTAL-EXPOSITION)，将 ESTABLISH idiom 扩展为 MOVEMENT-DRIVEN 单镜头模式。见 shot-logic.md。
**Pattern**: SINGLE-TAKE(PAN/TILT through environment -> character -> character's objects -> character's identity -> return to environment). Each resting point delivers one exposition unit. The return to the starting frame closes the loop.
**Parameters**:
- `n_exposition_units`: 5-12 (each pan-stop delivers one fact)
- `duration`: 30s-180s
- `movement_type`: PAN, TILT, or combination (no dolly -- the camera is positioned as the audience's fixed observation point)
- `closure_type`: CIRCULAR (return to start) or LINEAR (end at character)
- `dialogue`: NONE (pure visual storytelling)
- `info_density_per_unit`: exactly 1-2 facts per resting point
- `causal_chain`: each unit must logically connect to the next (A explains B explains C)
**Emotional Effect**: The audience becomes a detective, assembling a character profile from visual evidence. Creates intimacy without dialogue. The continuous movement creates an unbroken contract of trust with the viewer.
**When to Use**: Character introductions. Establishing scenes where environment IS character. Opening sequences. Any moment where you want the audience to actively read the image rather than passively receive information.
**When NOT to Use**: Scenes requiring dialogue. Scenes with multiple characters in dynamic interaction. High-action sequences. Scenes where the audience already knows the character.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A02**: Our idiom FSM for ESTABLISH assumes discrete shots (ELS->LS->MS). A continuous single-take establish has no representation in the idiom system. The states are the same but the transitions are camera movements within a shot rather than cuts between shots.
- **Proposed fix**: Allow idiom FSMs to operate in two modes: CUT-DRIVEN (standard) and MOVEMENT-DRIVEN (single-take). In MOVEMENT-DRIVEN mode, state transitions are triggered by camera movement resting points rather than edits. Add `idiom_mode: CUT | MOVEMENT` parameter to all idiom definitions.

#### Logic Analysis

- **Cut reasons**: The title sequence uses cuts as INFORMATION exclusively but the info is micro-fragmented: each ECU delivers one obsessive detail. The crime scene uses a standard ESTABLISH->INVESTIGATE pattern but every shot has INFO_DENSITY >= 3 (multiple visual details crammed into each frame).
- **Information flow**: Title sequence: parallel, non-sequential DAG (any order would work -- this is THEMATIC montage, not narrative). Crime scene: strict sequential DAG with Somerset as the reader-surrogate -- we discover as he discovers.
- **Idioms used**: Crime scene uses REVEAL-PROGRESSIVE (standard) but with abnormally high INFO_DENSITY per shot. Title sequence uses no standard idiom -- it is pure tonal/thematic montage (Eisenstein Level 3: Tonal).

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T03 -- Information Overload Reveal
**Idiom映射**: 此技法扩展了 REVEAL-PROGRESSIVE idiom 的 HIGH-DENSITY 变体，同时提出了新 Idiom (THEMATIC-MONTAGE) 用于片头序列。见 shot-logic.md。
**Pattern**: ESTABLISH(exterior, cold) -> APPROACH(corridor, steady follow, increasing grime) -> THRESHOLD(door opens, first glimpse, slow push) -> SCAN(methodical CU/INSERT cycle, each shot contains 3+ visual details) -> REACTION-PAIR(professional + novice, contrasted) -> HIDDEN-CLUE(partially obscured, found by methodical character) -> STEP-BACK(MS, character processing)
**Parameters**:
- `info_density_per_shot`: 3-6 (vs normal 1-2). Each frame is packed with visual information
- `color_desaturation`: 40-80% (sickly, institutional, drained of life)
- `approach_duration`: 15-30s of corridor/transition before the reveal (builds dread through environment)
- `scan_pattern`: CU/INSERT alternating with REACTION shots (detective reads scene, we read detective)
- `hidden_clue_visibility`: 10-30% frame occupancy (present but not foregrounded)
- `reaction_contrast`: pair two characters with different emotional registers (professional calm vs shocked novice)
**Emotional Effect**: The audience is overwhelmed by detail, mirroring the detective's information processing challenge. The packed frames reward re-watching. The desaturation removes beauty, forcing engagement with content over aesthetics.
**When to Use**: Crime scene investigations. Discovering complex environments for the first time. Any scene where a character must "read" a space full of meaning. Establishing a villain's psychology through their environment.
**When NOT to Use**: Simple discoveries (one object, one reveal). Action sequences. Scenes where emotional clarity matters more than informational density. Romance.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A03a**: INFO_DENSITY in our framework has a recommended max of 5 per shot (shot-logic.md). Fincher routinely runs at 4-6 per shot for entire sequences. The framework should allow genre-specific INFO_DENSITY ranges.
- **Proposed fix**: Add genre modifier to INFO_DENSITY in shot-logic.md: `ID_max(crime_investigation) = 6`, `ID_max(dialogue) = 3`, `ID_max(action) = 2`.

**GAP-A03b**: Title sequence montage is pure TONAL (Eisenstein Level 3) -- our idiom system only covers narrative-sequential idioms, not thematic montages where shot order is arbitrary.
- **Proposed fix**: Add THEMATIC-MONTAGE idiom to shot-logic.md: shots share a theme/mood but have no sequential dependency. DAG edges are THEMATIC (same mood) not CAUSAL (A enables B).

### A04: Zodiac -- Interrogation Scene

**Film**: Zodiac (2007)
**Director**: David Fincher | **Year**: 2007 | **Genre**: Crime Thriller
**Scene**: Robert Graysmith interviews Arthur Leigh Allen. ~8 minutes of sustained two-person dialogue with minimal camera movement. Tension built entirely through duration and performance.

#### Shot Breakdown (estimated key portion, ~4 minutes)

| # | Size | Angle | Movement | Duration | Content |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 3.0s | Room establishing: industrial, fluorescent, a table between two men |
| 2 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 12.0s | Graysmith asking questions -- nervous, trying to appear casual. Camera does not move. |
| 3 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 15.0s | Allen answering -- calm, amused, slightly condescending. Holds Graysmith's gaze. Camera does not move. |
| 4 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 8.0s | Graysmith -- swallows, looks down at notes, looks back up |
| 5 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 18.0s | Allen -- long answer, no cut away. He talks about his hobbies. Camera locked. The audience is trapped with his face. |
| 6 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 6.0s | Graysmith -- asks about the watch (key evidence). Voice slightly tighter. |
| 7 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 10.0s | Allen -- pauses. Three full seconds of silence before answering. "Which watch?" |
| 8 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 4.0s | Graysmith -- realizes the pause was significant. |
| 9 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 20.0s | Allen -- extended monologue about watches, collection, innocuous details. Duration is the weapon: we cannot escape his face. |
| 10 | CU | EYE | VERY SLOW PUSH | 8.0s | Graysmith -- imperceptible push-in over 8 seconds (the audience may not consciously notice the camera moving). Sweat visible. |
| 11 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 12.0s | Allen -- "Is there something you want to ask me?" Direct eye contact with camera axis (almost POV). |
| 12 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 5.0s | Graysmith -- frozen. The interview has reversed. |
| 13 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 4.0s | Two-shot reestablish. Physical distance between them. Allen leans back, relaxed. Graysmith rigid. |
| 14 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 8.0s | Allen smiles. |

**Average shot duration**: ~10.2 seconds. Total movement: one near-imperceptible push-in.

#### Logic Analysis

- **Cut reasons**: Almost exclusively REACTION (alternating who we watch listening). The few non-REACTION cuts are EMPHASIS (MCU->CU shift in shots 6-7 when the questioning gets dangerous). Shot 13 is ORIENTATION (reestablishing spatial relationship after extended CU sequence).
- **Information flow**: Linear, strictly causal. Each answer PROVIDES information that RAISES a new question, which the next question addresses. The DAG is a clean chain. But the *real* information is in the N-Channel: Allen's pauses, his comfort level, the power dynamic reversal.
- **Idioms used**: SHOT-REVERSE-SHOT, but radically extended. Standard SRS: 3-5s per shot. Fincher's SRS: 8-20s per shot. The idiom is the same structure, but the parameters are at 3-5x normal values.

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T04 -- Duration-as-Tension (Extended Shot-Reverse-Shot)
**Idiom映射**: 此技法扩展了 SHOT-REVERSE-SHOT idiom 的 EXTENDED 变体 (avg_duration 8-20s)，同时为 visual-rhythm.md 提出了 DELIBERATE-UNIFORM 节奏模式。
**Pattern**: ESTABLISH(room, MS, 1 shot) -> SRS-EXTENDED(MCU/CU alternation, each shot 8-20s, minimal movement) -> MICRO-ESCALATION(gradual CU push from MCU, almost imperceptible) -> POWER-REVERSAL(one character takes control via direct address/challenge) -> REESTABLISH(MS two-shot showing new spatial dynamic)
**Parameters**:
- `avg_shot_duration`: 8-20s (vs standard SRS 3-5s)
- `camera_movement`: ZERO or near-imperceptible push-in (<2mm/s equivalent)
- `escalation_via`: shot size change (MCU->CU) NOT cutting speed (speed stays constant)
- `silence_beats`: at least 2 pauses of 3+ seconds within the sequence
- `power_reversal_marker`: one character directly addresses/challenges the other, breaking the Q&A pattern
- `total_sequence_duration`: 3-10 minutes (the technique requires sustained commitment)
- `angle_variation`: ZERO (all shots at eye level, no Dutch, no low/high -- the flatness is the point)
**Emotional Effect**: The viewer becomes increasingly uncomfortable because the normal escape valves (cutaways, reaction shots, camera movement) are withheld. The locked camera forces confrontation. Duration itself becomes oppressive.
**When to Use**: Interrogation scenes. Job interviews. Confrontations where one character has power over another. Any dialogue scene where tension must build slowly and release is deliberately withheld.
**When NOT to Use**: Action. Scenes with more than 2 characters. Scenes where the audience needs spatial information. Scenes shorter than 2 minutes (the technique needs time to create its claustrophobic effect).

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A04**: Our rhythm analysis (visual-rhythm.md) treats long shot durations as problems ("节奏偏均匀"). But Fincher deliberately creates uniform long durations as a tension device. The framework needs to distinguish "unintentionally monotonous rhythm" from "deliberately oppressive rhythm."
- **Proposed fix**: Add DELIBERATE-UNIFORMITY as a valid rhythm mode in visual-rhythm.md. Diagnostic: if shot durations are uniform AND the scene involves sustained interpersonal tension, the uniformity may be intentional. Add: `rhythm_mode: VARIED (default) | PULSE | DELIBERATE-UNIFORM | ACCELERANDO | RITARDANDO`.

### A05: Oldboy -- Corridor Fight

**Film**: Oldboy (2003)
**Director**: Park Chan-wook | **Year**: 2003 | **Genre**: Action/Thriller
**Scene**: Oh Dae-su fights his way through a corridor full of thugs. Single continuous side-scrolling take, ~3 minutes.

#### Shot Breakdown (estimated)

This is a single continuous shot. I break it into phases defined by the action.

| # | Size | Angle | Movement | Duration | Content |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1a | LS | EYE (PROFILE) | STATIC | 5s | Dae-su at one end of corridor, thugs at the other. Flat side-on framing like a 2D game. |
| 1b | LS | EYE (PROFILE) | TRACK R | 15s | First engagement: Dae-su attacks with hammer. Sloppy, brutal, realistic fighting. Camera tracks right following him. Thugs fall, get back up. |
| 1c | LS | EYE (PROFILE) | TRACK L | 10s | Pushed back. Dae-su retreats. Camera tracks left. He's losing. Breathing hard. |

#### Logic Analysis

- **Cut reasons**: None (single take). The ABSENCE of cuts is the technique. Every standard storyboard would cut this into 30-50 shots with close-ups of hits, reaction shots, inserts of weapons. Park refuses all of that.
- **Information flow**: The single-take means the DAG is not shot-to-shot but moment-to-moment within the shot. The spatial information is always complete (we see the entire corridor width). The audience always knows how many enemies remain, how far Dae-su has progressed, how exhausted everyone is.
- **Idioms used**: This is an anti-CHASE idiom. Where CHASE accelerates cutting, this DECELERATES to zero cuts. Where CHASE fragments space, this preserves complete spatial integrity. Where CHASE builds urgency through rhythm, this builds urgency through exhaustion.
- **Key insight**: The side-scrolling profile framing is a deliberate reference to video game side-scrollers. It is simultaneously realistic (no editing trickery) and artificial (theatrical staging in a realistic space).

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T05 -- Exhaustion Long Take (Side-Scroll Action)
**Idiom映射**: 此技法提出了新 Idiom (LONG-TAKE-ACTION)，具有独立的内部阶段 FSM，不同于现有的 CHASE idiom。见 shot-logic.md。
**Pattern**: SINGLE-TAKE(PROFILE framing, lateral tracking only) with internal phases: CONFRONTATION-ESTABLISH -> PUSH-FORWARD -> PUSH-BACK -> PAUSE(exhaustion visible) -> PUSH-FORWARD-AGAIN(diminished) -> ARRIVAL. Camera movement direction mirrors protagonist's progress.
**Parameters**:
- `framing`: strictly PROFILE (side-on). No frontal, no three-quarter. The audience is positioned as a theater viewer.
- `camera_movement`: LATERAL TRACK only. Direction follows protagonist.
- `shot_duration`: 60-240s (minimum 60s for exhaustion to register)
- `pause_beat`: required. At least one 5-10s pause where all combatants stop and breathe. This is where the audience feels the weight.
- `fighting_realism`: HIGH. Sloppy, inefficient, people slip, get up, fall again. No choreographed elegance.
- `spatial_completeness`: the full action space is always visible. Audience can count remaining opponents.
- `escalation_via`: degradation of fighting quality (fresh at start, barely standing at end), not editing speed
**Emotional Effect**: The viewer experiences the physical toll of violence. The refusal to cut prevents emotional escape. The pause beat is devastating -- it reveals that this is not an action fantasy but a depiction of exhaustion and desperation.
**When to Use**: Fight scenes where physical cost matters. Scenes emphasizing endurance. Any violence that should feel heavy rather than exciting. Breakthrough moments (literally fighting through to reach something).
**When NOT to Use**: Fights that should feel exciting/kinetic (use rapid cutting instead). Scenes with complex spatial layouts (corridor is constrained 2D space). Scenes involving guns (the pause beat doesn't work with ranged weapons). Short fights.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A05**: Our framework treats single-take sequences as a variant of the ESTABLISH idiom. But the Oldboy corridor is not establishing -- it is NARRATIVE ACTION in a single take. We need a formal category for single-take action sequences with their own internal phase structure.
- **Proposed fix**: Add LONG-TAKE-ACTION as a new idiom family in shot-logic.md with internal phase FSM: `CONFRONT -> {ADVANCE | RETREAT}* -> PAUSE -> {ADVANCE | RETREAT}* -> RESOLVE`. Parameters: framing axis (PROFILE/FRONTAL/FOLLOWING), movement axis (LATERAL/DEPTH/CIRCULAR), pause requirement (mandatory/optional).

#### Logic Analysis

- **Cut reasons**: The hallway sequences are overwhelmingly MOVEMENT-DRIVEN (no cuts during the following shots). - **Idioms used**: No standard idiom applies. This is not ESTABLISH (we never understand the full space -- that's the point). Not CHASE (no acceleration). Not SRS. This is a new pattern.

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T06 -- Architectural Stalking (Following Through Geometric Space)
**Idiom映射**: 此技法提出了新 Idiom (ARCHITECTURAL-STALKING)，并要求 shot-logic.md 增加 `spatial_comprehension_goal: LOW` 参数。本质上是 M12 (走廊=不可逃避) 隐喻的镜头实现。
**Pattern**: FOLLOW(Steadicam behind character, geometric space, repetitive architecture) -> REPETITION(turns revealing identical spaces) -> DISRUPTION(sudden static shot breaking the following pattern -- anomaly in the space) -> FLASH(optional rapid inserts of horror/memory) -> RESUME-FOLLOW(return to following, normalcy restored, but the audience is now afraid of every corner)
**Parameters**:
- `camera_position`: BEHIND character, SLIGHTLY LOW (Kubrick consistently positions camera at child-height even when following adults in the maze)
- `camera_movement`: STEADICAM only (not handheld -- the supernatural smoothness IS the horror. A ghost's perspective.)
- `space_geometry`: SYMMETRICAL. Identical hallways. Parallel lines converging at vanishing points. Repeated patterns.
- `repetition_count`: 2-4 turns before disruption (enough to establish false pattern)
- `disruption_type`: STATIC shot (breaking the movement contract) containing anomaly
- `flash_duration`: 0.5-2.0s (intrusions of horror)
- `sound_design`: CRITICAL. The rhythmic sound of movement (Big Wheel wheels on carpet/wood) creates a pulse that the disruption breaks.
- `spatial_comprehension`: deliberately LOW. The audience should NOT be able to map the space. The hotel/maze is unknowable.
**Emotional Effect**: The smooth, relentless forward movement creates the feeling of being pulled toward something inevitable. The geometric symmetry is oppressive -- beauty as prison. The disruption shots are terrifying precisely because the preceding uniformity makes any anomaly catastrophic.
**When to Use**: Horror (haunted spaces). Psychological thriller (institutional spaces -- hospitals, prisons, hotels). Any scene where architecture itself is the antagonist. Transition sequences that need to build dread rather than simply connect locations.
**When NOT to Use**: Realistic settings where spatial comprehension matters. Scenes requiring multiple characters. Dialogue scenes. Any scene where the audience needs to trust the space.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A06**: Our framework has no concept of SPATIAL INCOMPREHENSION as a deliberate strategy. The ORIENTATION cut reason assumes the goal is always to maintain spatial coherence. Kubrick deliberately destroys spatial coherence to create horror.
- **Proposed fix**: Add `spatial_comprehension_goal: HIGH (default) | LOW (deliberate disorientation)` parameter to the ORIENTATION system in shot-logic.md. When set to LOW, the absence of ORIENTATION cuts is intentional, not an error. The spatial confusion IS the technique.

- `vertical_direction`: DOWN (descent = decline) or UP (ascent = aspiration). Parasite uses DOWN.
- `n_descent_stages`: 4-6 (each visually distinct but linked by the action of descending stairs)
- `camera_angle`: consistently HIGH for descent (looking down on characters = the world looking down on them). LOW for the final interior (looking up = we are at the bottom now).

- `weather_as_equalizer`: same weather event experienced differently by different classes
- `contrast_intercuts`: 2-3 shots of privileged space experiencing same conditions as pleasant
- `culmination_object`: one symbolic object at the bottom (the scholar's rock, floating in sewage -- aspiration literally drowning)

- `duration_ratio`: pre-violence avg / violence avg should be 3-5x (the contrast is the weapon)
- `sound_design`: near-SILENCE during pre-violence. Ambient only. No score. The absence of music is the score.
- `n_threat_elements`: 3-5 environmental details that accumulate (traffic, exposure, suspect car, civilians as shields)
- `protagonist_comprehension`: protagonist does NOT fully understand the situation (audience sees the trap before the protagonist does -- Hitchcockian)
- `wide_shot_percentage`: >60% of shots are LS or wider (the space is the threat)

The long holds on wide shots create maximum visual vulnerability -- there's no close-up to hide behind. The sudden violence is devastating because of the rhythm contract violation.
**When to Use**: Convoy/journey scenes through hostile territory. Any sequence where the environment itself is the threat. Scenes requiring sustained tension without dialogue. Military/police operations. Border crossings, checkpoints, contested spaces.
**When NOT to Use**: Indoor scenes (needs landscape scale). Scenes requiring dialogue-driven tension (use T04 instead). Scenes where the protagonist is in control. Comedic or romantic contexts.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A08**: Our framework's tension model (visual-rhythm.md) focuses on DURATION CONTRACTION (shots getting shorter = more tense). Villeneuve demonstrates DURATION EXPANSION as tension -- shots getting LONGER creates dread because the audience waits for the cut that will release them. We model acceleration but not sustained deceleration-as-tension.
- **Proposed fix**: Add EXPANSION-DREAD to the rhythm modes in visual-rhythm.md: `rhythm_intent: ACCELERATION (urgency) | DECELERATION (release) | EXPANSION-DREAD (shots get longer, tension builds through withheld release) | DELIBERATE-UNIFORM (oppressive)`. But the standard cut-reason framework misses the essential logic: each iteration PROVIDES "the same encounter with one variable changed." The cut reason is VARIATION.
- **Information flow**: The DAG across iterations is not sequential but LAYERED. Each iteration has the same V-PROVIDES (staircase, two people, passing) but different N-PROVIDES (strangers -> acquaintances -> potential lovers). The RAISES are the same each time ("will they act on their feelings?") but the emotional charge increases.
- **Idioms used**: No standard idiom. This is a LOOP structure, not a linear progression.

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T09 -- Repetition with Variation (Temporal Loop)
**Idiom映射**: 此技法提出了新 Idiom (VARIATION-LOOP)，要求 shot-logic.md 增加 `idiom_topology: LOOP` 参数——这是框架中首个非线性拓扑的 idiom。
**Pattern**: ENCOUNTER-V1(location A, characters pass, minimal interaction, distinctive visual marker) -> TIME-SKIP -> ENCOUNTER-V2(SAME location, SAME angle, SAME music, but ONE variable changed: warmer interaction) -> TIME-SKIP -> ENCOUNTER-V3(SAME everything, but another variable changed: more connection) -> [N iterations]. Each iteration is a near-identical shot sequence with ONE thing different.
**Parameters**:
- `n_iterations`: 3-6 (3 minimum for pattern recognition, 6 maximum before repetition becomes tedious)
- `fixed_elements`: location, camera angle, music cue, movement direction (these MUST be identical)
- `variable_element`: ONE thing changes per iteration (body language, proximity, dialogue presence, touch)
- `slow_motion_factor`: 50-70% speed (stretches time, makes each moment precious)
- `step_printing`: optional -- repeating frames to create a stutter/dreamlike quality (Wong's signature)
- `frame_within_frame`: doorways, railings, walls used to frame characters within the wider frame (entrapment, separation)
- `dialogue_audibility`: LOW to NONE (music replaces dialogue -- what they say matters less than that they're together)
- `color_temperature`: WARM (amber, red, gold -- the color of memory and desire)
- `visual_marker`: costume change or prop change between iterations to signal time passing (Mrs. Chan's cheongsam patterns)
**Emotional Effect**: The repetition creates ritual. The variations within repetition create an ache -- the audience recognizes the pattern and anticipates the next change. Each iteration carries the accumulated emotional weight of all previous iterations. The fixed elements become sacred (their staircase, their song, their alley). The slow motion stretches moments that are fleeting in reality, giving them the weight of myth.
**When to Use**: Romance (growing attraction). Obsession sequences. Memory/nostalgia montages. Any narrative where the relationship between two elements develops through repeated encounters. Seasonal passage-of-time sequences.
**When NOT to Use**: Thriller (unless depicting obsessive surveillance pattern). Action. Any scene requiring narrative progression through new events rather than deepening of existing patterns. Sequences where dialogue content matters.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A09**: Our idiom FSMs are strictly LINEAR (state A -> state B -> state C). They have no concept of LOOPS (state A -> A' -> A'' where the prime marks represent variations). The Repetition-with-Variation structure is a loop idiom, not a linear one.
- **Proposed fix**: Add LOOP idiom type to shot-logic.md: `idiom_topology: LINEAR (default) | LOOP(n_iterations, fixed_set, variable_set)`. Define loop entry/exit conditions and the per-iteration variable change rule.

### A10: No Country for Old Men -- Coin Toss

**Film**: No Country for Old Men (2007)
**Director**: Joel & Ethan Coen | **Year**: 2007 | **Genre**: Thriller/Crime
**Scene**: Anton Chigurh stops at a gas station and engages the elderly proprietor in a conversation that gradually reveals itself as a life-or-death gamble. ~4.5 minutes.

#### Shot Breakdown (estimated)

| # | Size | Angle | Movement | Duration | Content |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | LS | EYE | STATIC | 3.0s | Gas station exterior. Desolate. Chigurh's car. Wind. |
| 2 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 2.5s | Interior: proprietor behind counter. Quiet day. |
| 3 | MS | EYE | STATIC | 2.0s | Chigurh enters. Walks to counter. |

| 18 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 8.0s | Chigurh: "Yes." Dead stare. The longest hold on his face. The word "yes" carries the weight of murder. |
| 19 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 4.0s | Proprietor: "I need to know what I stand to win." Desperate. |
| 20 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 10.0s | Chigurh: "Everything." Pause. "How's that?" Another pause. "You stand to win everything. Call it." |
| 21 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 3.0s | Proprietor: "...Heads." |
| 22 | INSERT | EYE | STATIC | 2.0s | Coin uncovered. |
| 23 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 4.0s | Chigurh: relief? satisfaction? unreadable. "Well done." |

| 25 | MCU | EYE | STATIC | 2.0s | Proprietor: shaking. Takes coin. |
| 26 | LS | EYE | STATIC | 4.0s | Chigurh leaves. Gas station. Silence. |

#### Logic Analysis

- **Cut reasons**: 100% REACTION in the SRS alternation. Every single cut is motivated by "show the other person's response to what was just said." The escalation is entirely in the N-Channel: the same SRS structure carries increasingly lethal subtext. The shot size changes (MCU->CU in shots 9 and 11) are EMPHASIS but so subtle they might be missed.
- **Information flow**: The V-Channel is nearly EMPTY (two men, a counter, a gas station -- nothing visual changes). The A-Channel carries all literal information (dialogue). The N-Channel carries all narrative tension (the gradual realization that this is a death threat). The DAG is dominated by N-PROVIDES and N-RAISES: each line of dialogue shifts the power dynamic.
- **Idioms used**: Textbook SHOT-REVERSE-SHOT but with three key modifications:
  1. Asymmetric duration: Chigurh's shots are consistently 1.5-2x longer than the proprietor's
  2. Asymmetric shot size: Chigurh gets CU, proprietor stays MCU (closer = more powerful/threatening)
  3. No master/reestablishing shot after opening (the spatial relationship is irrelevant -- only the faces matter)

#### Extracted Technique

**Name**: T10 -- Mundane Lethality (Micro-Escalation Dialogue)
**Idiom映射**: 此技法扩展了 SHOT-REVERSE-SHOT idiom 的 ASYMMETRIC 变体 (size_offset + duration_ratio 编码权力差异)。见 shot-logic.md。
**Pattern**: ESTABLISH(mundane setting, 1-2 shots) -> SRS-ASYMMETRIC(character A in CU with longer holds, character B in MCU with shorter holds) -> MICRO-ESCALATION(each exchange subtly shifts from normal to threatening, escalation is in subtext not text) -> OBJECT-INTRODUCTION(a prop crystallizes the threat -- coin, knife, phone, document) -> CLIMAX-HOLD(longest single shot on threatening character) -> RELEASE(mundane action -- leaving, putting something away -- but the audience knows death was present)
**Parameters**:
- `setting_mundanity`: HIGH (gas station, diner, laundromat -- the more ordinary, the more disturbing)
- `duration_asymmetry`: threatening character's shots 1.5-2.5x longer than victim's
- `size_asymmetry`: threatening character one size closer (CU vs MCU)
- `escalation_per_exchange`: 5-15% increase in threat level per SRS cycle (not sudden -- gradient)
- `n_exchanges_before_object`: 4-8 (enough to establish unease before crystallizing it)
- `object_symbolic_weight`: the prop must be ordinary (coin, not gun) but charged with lethal meaning by context
- `dialogue_surface_meaning`: remains conversational/mundane throughout (weather, closing time, names)
- `dialogue_subtext`: shifts from innocent to lethal without the surface text changing register
- `climax_hold_duration`: longest single shot in sequence (8-12s) on threatening character's face after the ultimatum
- `no_violence`: the scene ends without physical violence (the threat is sufficient; actualization would diminish it)
**Emotional Effect**: Terror through normalcy. The most frightening scenes are ones where death enters through the vocabulary of small talk. The asymmetric SRS makes the audience unconsciously adopt the victim's position (we spend less time with them, they have less power). The mundane setting strips away genre armor -- this could happen anywhere.
**When to Use**: Scenes introducing psychopathic/sociopathic characters. Confrontations where one character has total power. Scenes where the threat must remain ambiguous (is this a real threat or am I misreading?). Any dialogue where subtext carries more weight than text.
**When NOT to Use**: Scenes between equals. Scenes requiring physical action. Scenes where the threat is already explicit (no need for subtext when guns are drawn). Scenes with more than 2 characters.

#### Framework Gap

**GAP-A10**: Our SRS idiom FSM treats all participants as symmetric (equal shot sizes, equal durations). The Coen Brothers demonstrate that ASYMMETRIC SRS is a distinct power-dynamic tool. Shot size and duration encode dominance.
- **Proposed fix**: Add asymmetry parameters to the SRS idiom in shot-logic.md: `srs_symmetry: SYMMETRIC (default, equal sizes/durations) | ASYMMETRIC(dominant_char, size_offset, duration_ratio)`. When ASYMMETRIC, the dominant character gets closer framing and longer holds. The asymmetry should be flagged with a narrative justification (power differential, threat, authority).

### M01: Stalker -- Zone Entry

**Film**: Stalker (1979)
**Director**: Andrei Tarkovsky | **Year**: 1979 | **Genre**: Art Film/Sci-Fi

**Scene**: The three characters enter the Zone on a railcar. A single take lasting approximately 4 minutes, then a series of long takes averaging 60+ seconds inside the Zone.

**Key Shots (estimated)**:

| # | Size | Angle | Movement | Duration | Content |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | CU | EYE | STATIC (train vibration) | 240s | Three faces in extreme close-up on the railcar. Camera barely moves. Faces fill the frame. Sound of rails. Color shifts from sepia to saturated green as they enter the Zone. The color change happens within the continuous shot. |
| 2 | LS | EYE | SLOW PAN | 90s | First view of the Zone: overgrown landscape, rusted vehicles, water. Camera pans slowly across. No human movement. |
| 3 | ECU | BIRD | STATIC | 60s | Objects underwater: a syringe, a religious icon, coins, a gun. The camera simply looks down at them. No narration. |

**Technique Extracted**: M01-T -- Contemplative Duration
- **Idiom映射**: 此技法为 visual-rhythm.md 提出了 CONTEMPLATIVE 节奏模式，暂停所有时长上限规则。不映射到现有 Idiom——超越叙事性框架。
- **Pattern**: SINGLE-TAKE(extreme duration, minimal movement, subject fills frame) -> the shot outlasts the viewer's narrative expectations -> the viewer stops waiting for the cut -> a new mode of perception activates (meditative, contemplative)
- **Parameters**: `duration`: >60s per shot. `movement`: <5% frame change. `subject`: faces or landscapes. `transition_cue`: environmental (color change, sound change) NOT editorial (cut).
- **Emotional Effect**: Transcendence. The viewer surrenders narrative expectation and enters a state of pure observation. Time itself becomes the subject.
- **When to Use**: Transitions between narrative modes (ordinary world -> special world). Meditation scenes. Death scenes. Any moment requiring the audience to leave narrative processing and enter contemplative processing.
- **Framework Gap**: Our rhythm system has no concept of shots that outlast narrative -- shots whose purpose is to EXHAUST the viewer's narrative-seeking impulse. The minimum-duration guidelines in visual-rhythm.md (CU max ~15s) would flag all Tarkovsky as errors.
  - **Proposed fix**: Add `rhythm_intent: NARRATIVE (default) | CONTEMPLATIVE` mode. In CONTEMPLATIVE mode, duration guidelines are suspended and the quality metric becomes "does the viewer's perception mode shift?" rather than "is information being delivered?"

### M02: Dunkirk -- Cross-cutting

**Film**: Dunkirk (2017)
**Director**: Christopher Nolan | **Year**: 2017 | **Genre**: War/Thriller

**Scene**: The entire film's structure -- three timelines running at different temporal scales (one week on the beach, one day on the sea, one hour in the air) intercut to create simultaneous tension.

**Key Structural Pattern (estimated)**:

| Timeline | Scale | ASL | Shot Style | Content |
|----------|-------|-----|------------|---------|
| The Mole (land) | 1 week | 3-4s | MS/LS, handheld, chaotic | Soldiers waiting for evacuation, bombings, drowning |
| The Sea | 1 day | 4-6s | MS, steadier, slightly more traditional | Civilian boat crossing Channel, rescue attempts |
| The Air | 1 hour | 5-8s | CU in cockpit, ELS exteriors, precise | Spitfire pilot in combat, fuel gauge counting down |

**Cross-cut structure in climactic sequence**:

| # | Timeline | Size | Duration | Content |
|---|----------|------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Air | CU | 3s | Pilot checking fuel gauge -- almost empty |
| 2 | Mole | MS | 2s | Soldiers on sinking ship, water rising |
| 3 | Sea | LS | 4s | Civilian boat approaching the soldiers |
| 4 | Air | ELS | 3s | Spitfire banking toward the beach |
| 5 | Mole | CU | 1.5s | Soldier's face underwater |
| 6 | Sea | MS | 2s | Man reaching hand down to pull someone up |
| 7 | Air | CU | 2s | Pilot's hand on throttle, engine coughs |
| 8 | Mole | LS | 1.5s | Ship rolling over |

**Technique Extracted**: M02-T -- Triple Timeline Weave
- **Idiom映射**: 此技法扩展了 MONTAGE (Parallel) idiom 至 N-timeline 结构，要求 montage-theory.md 增加 convergence_point 参数。
- **Pattern**: Establish three independent timelines with different temporal scales -> intercut them so they CONVERGE at the climax even though they cover different durations -> each timeline has its own rhythm (land=chaotic, sea=steady, air=precise) -> cross-cutting accelerates as convergence approaches -> at the climax point, all three timelines meet in the same moment
- **Parameters**: `n_timelines`: 2-4. `temporal_scale_ratio`: timelines must cover different durations (1 week / 1 day / 1 hour). `convergence_point`: the moment all timelines share. `per_timeline_ASL`: each timeline has distinct rhythm. `cross_cut_frequency`: increases as convergence approaches (from every 30s to every 2-3s).
- **Emotional Effect**: The different temporal scales create a layered tension structure. The audience knows these timelines will converge but not exactly when. Each timeline ratchets tension independently. The convergence moment is overwhelming because three streams of tension release simultaneously.
- **Framework Gap**: Our montage theory (montage-theory.md) covers parallel montage (two timelines) but not three or more. The temporal scale difference (same edit connects moments separated by days) has no formal representation.
  - **Proposed fix**: Extend parallel montage in montage-theory.md to support N-timeline structures with explicit temporal_scale per timeline and a formal convergence_point where DAGs merge.

### M03: Inglourious Basterds -- Tavern Scene

**Film**: Inglourious Basterds (2009)
**Director**: Quentin Tarantino | **Year**: 2009 | **Genre**: Thriller/War

**Scene**: The basement tavern scene. ~25 minutes of sustained social tension where British soldiers disguised as Germans play a card game with an SS officer who gradually suspects them. The scene turns on a single wrong hand gesture.

**Key Pattern (estimated composite)**:

| Phase | Duration | ASL | Shot Style | Tension Source |
|-------|----------|-----|------------|---------------|
| Establishing | ~3 min | 5-7s | MS/LS, multiple characters, warm lighting | Social situation, everyone playing roles |
| Card Game | ~8 min | 6-10s | MCU/CU SRS between players, tightening | Verbal sparring disguised as small talk, the SS officer asking pointed questions |
| Suspicion Rising | ~6 min | 8-15s | CU holds getting longer, almost Fincher-like (T04) | The officer notices something wrong but doesn't reveal what |
| The Gesture | ~1 min | 2-4s | INSERT of hand signal (three fingers held wrong), CU reactions | The mistake is made. Everyone at the table knows. |
| Standoff | ~3 min | 3-5s | Under-table shots (guns pointed), CU faces, no one moves | Mexican standoff. All pretense dropped. |
| Violence | ~15s | 0.5-1.5s | Rapid cutting, fragmentation (echoes T01) | Explosive gunfight lasting seconds after 20+ minutes of tension |

**Technique Extracted**: M03-T -- Sustained Social Tension (The Long Fuse)
- **Idiom映射**: 此技法扩展了 SHOT-REVERSE-SHOT idiom 的累积变体，要求 shot-logic.md 增加 `n_channel_mode: CUMULATIVE` 参数。暴力释放段落复用 T01 (FRAGMENTATION-ASSAULT)。
- **Pattern**: Establish social scene with hidden threat -> gradually extend shot durations as suspicion builds -> introduce micro-clues through INSERT shots -> reach revelation via a single small detail (the gesture, the wrong word, the tell) -> shift to standoff with under-surface shots (literally showing what's hidden) -> explosive release (violence) lasting <5% of the scene's duration
- **Parameters**: `buildup_duration`: 80-95% of total scene time. `release_duration`: 5-20% of total. `ASL_progression`: starts at 5-7s, peaks at 10-15s during maximum tension, drops to <2s for violence. `hidden_weapon`: literal (under-table guns) and figurative (knowledge, suspicion). `revelation_trigger`: ONE small detail that is trivially small but narratively catastrophic. `post_revelation_duration`: kept SHORT -- once the truth is known, violence follows quickly.
- **Emotional Effect**: The extreme duration ratio (20 min tension : 15 sec violence) makes the audience internalize the characters' experience of waiting. Every pleasantry is loaded. The single wrong gesture retroactively charges every previous moment with danger.
- **Framework Gap**: Our framework doesn't model ACCUMULATING SUBTEXT across a long sequence. Each shot's N-PROVIDES is evaluated independently, but Tarantino's technique requires tracking cumulative N-PROVIDES across 20+ minutes where each new piece of subtext amplifies all previous subtext exponentially rather than additively.
  - **Proposed fix**: Add `n_channel_mode: INDEPENDENT (default) | CUMULATIVE` to the information DAG model. In CUMULATIVE mode, N-PROVIDES values compound: the Nth piece of subtext has weight N, not 1. This models the exponential tension buildup.

### M04: Get Out -- Sunken Place

**Film**: Get Out (2017)
**Director**: Jordan Peele | **Year**: 2017 | **Genre**: Horror/Thriller

**Scene**: Chris is hypnotized by Missy Armitage and enters the "Sunken Place" -- a visual metaphor for the Black American experience of being silenced. His consciousness falls away from his body into a black void while watching his own life through a small screen.

**Key Shots (estimated)**:

| # | Size | Angle | Movement | Duration | Content |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 8s | Missy stirring tea cup. Sound of spoon on porcelain -- hypnotic. |
| 2 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 6s | Chris's face. A tear runs down. He cannot move. |
| 3 | CU | EYE | STATIC | 4s | Missy: "Sink." |
| 4 | MS | BIRD | PULL BACK (VFX) | 8s | Chris falling backward in slow motion into blackness. The chair, the room, reality recedes above him. He falls into a void. The room becomes a small rectangle of light above -- a screen. |
| 5 | LS | LOW | STATIC | 6s | Chris floating in black void, the "screen" of reality above him showing the room he was in. He is watching his own life from below, unable to act. |
| 6 | CU | LOW | STATIC | 4s | Chris's face in the void -- mouth open in silent scream. |
| 7 | LS | EYE (from void) | STATIC | 5s | The "screen" above -- Missy visible, speaking, but sound is muffled. Chris cannot reach it. |

**Technique Extracted**: M04-T -- Visual Metaphor as Narrative Reality
- **Idiom映射**: 此技法提出了新 Idiom (REALITY-BREACH)，要求 shot-grammar.md 增加 `reality_mode: METAPHORICAL` 参数。本质上是将 visual-metaphor.md 的隐喻系统从"暗喻"升级为"实体化隐喻空间"。
- **Pattern**: REALITY(mundane setting, grounded camera, naturalistic) -> TRIGGER(a specific sensory cue -- sound, word, gesture) -> BREACH(VFX-driven transition from reality to metaphor space, usually involving falling/sinking/floating) -> METAPHOR-SPACE(the abstract concept made physically navigable -- the void, the prison, the mirror world) -> PERSPECTIVE-SHIFT(the character now watches reality from within the metaphor, literalizing their powerlessness)
- **Parameters**: `metaphor_physicality`: the abstract concept must be rendered as a physical space the character inhabits. `reality_window`: a visible connection to the real world (the "screen" above Chris). `sensory_trigger`: a specific repeatable stimulus that activates the transition (the tea cup sound for Chris). `return_mechanism`: how the character exits the metaphor space.
- **Emotional Effect**: By giving an abstract experience (marginalization, silencing, loss of agency) a physical architecture, the audience can FEEL what the character experiences. The "sunken place" is more powerful than any dialogue about oppression because it makes the audience physically experience it through spatial disorientation and scale.
- **Framework Gap**: Our framework operates entirely in physically plausible camera space. It has no model for VFX-driven transitions to metaphorical spaces or for shots that exist in subjective/psychological reality rather than physical reality.
  - **Proposed fix**: Add `reality_mode: PHYSICAL (default) | SUBJECTIVE | METAPHORICAL` to shot parameters in shot-grammar.md. SUBJECTIVE shots represent character perception (existing concept: POV). METAPHORICAL shots represent abstract concepts rendered as space (new concept). In METAPHORICAL mode, spatial coherence rules (180-degree, 30-degree, spatial matching) are suspended because the space follows emotional logic, not physical logic.

## Application to Mirror Visitor Ch1 (R085-R086)

The following techniques from the library are directly applicable to the Mirror Visitor chapter 1 storyboard:

### Applicable Techniques

1. **T04 Duration-as-Tension** -- The Lin Xiaoman / Ye Zhiqiu consultation scene is structurally identical to the Zodiac interrogation: two people, one table, escalating revelation. Apply:
   - Extended SRS with 8-15s holds on reaction shots
   - Imperceptible push-in when Xiaoman reveals the stalker's knowledge of therapy content
   - Shot 7 (ECU on Ye Zhiqiu's eyes) should be the longest hold in the sequence (10-12s)

2. **T10 Mundane Lethality (Asymmetric SRS)** -- Apply asymmetric sizing to the consultation: Ye Zhiqiu in MCU (the professional), Lin Xiaoman in CU (the vulnerable one). But REVERSE the asymmetry when the revelation hits: Xiaoman's accusation ("he said your mother's exact words") puts HER in power, so switch her to MCU (distance, accusation) and Ye Zhiqiu to ECU (trapped, exposed).

3. **T03 Information Overload** -- The "grey dust on feet" discovery sequence can use Fincher's scan technique:
   - Packed INSERT shots of Ye Zhiqiu's apartment showing disorder (medications, note fragments, unwashed dishes) at INFO_DENSITY 3-4
   - Let the audience read the space before Ye Zhiqiu does

4. **T09 Repetition with Variation** -- The "I didn't sleep well again" opening could establish a variation loop:
   - V1: Wake up, routine, go to office (normal day)
   - V2: Same shots, same angles, but one thing different (the dust on the feet, the cancelled appointment)
   - V3 is the disruption: Lin Xiaoman arrives and the pattern breaks

### Parameter Refinements from Application

- T04's `silence_beats` parameter works well at 2 beats minimum for the consultation. Testing suggests 3 beats for maximum effect in Chinese-language psychological drama where silence carries higher cultural weight.
- T10's `duration_asymmetry` of 1.5-2.5x should be pushed to 2.0-2.5x for the Mirror Visitor context because the power differential between therapist and client is deeply institutionalized.
- T03's `color_desaturation` should be LOWER (20-30%) for Mirror Visitor than for Se7en (60-80%) because the horror is psychological, not physical. The apartment should look "slightly wrong" not "nightmarish."
